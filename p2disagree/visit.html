You count to five in your head and slip into the virtual world you own for the day.


You hear birds chirping, but it doesn't sound like a song. It sounds a like a sound byte. The same chirping loops, and if you listen carefully, you can tell exactly when the sound of the wind starts over. Every 20 seconds.


The porch is nice enough. The dirt out front is dug to shreds, but then again, Nana always like gardening. Could the plants grow here? You start to wonder if their life is like Nana's. You turn to face the door, which is an eerily clean white. Finally, you knock.


Nana opens the door immediately. Her eyes are bright, but not like fire. Too... predictable. You shuffle your shoes on the doormat, which feels silly yet neccessary at the same time.


The pleasantries are nice enough. She tells you she's proud of you, she thanks you because you understand. You start to relax -- somehow she seemed okay.


She glances at you, and almost like she read your mind, she says, "I'm okay. I'm tired, I'm bored, but I guess I'm alive."


Her voice shook when she said "alive." Or maybe the audio just cut out. Maybe you'll always wonder.
